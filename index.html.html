The bowl is now half the day. At 570 and 750 it leaves 630 and 850 respectively for everything else — which is a workable lunch plus a small snack, and not much slack. Two levers if it gets tight: granola at ½ cup is ~220 kcal, and the second tablespoon of peanut butter is ~95. Cutting granola to ¼ cup on either bowl buys back a real snack without touching the açaí, berries, or chia — the parts actually doing the fiber work.

How the prep actually runs

Sunday · ~90 min

Batch day

1. Start the starch first — rice, potatoes, or quinoa. 2. Brown both ground meats in the same pan, meal A then meal B. 3. Roast or steam vegetables while the meat cooks. 4. Cool everything uncovered, then refrigerate overnight before portioning.

Why the cooling matters: cooked-and-cooled rice, potatoes, and quinoa form retrograded resistant starch — the main substrate your gut bacteria ferment into butyrate. Reheating gently is fine; the resistant starch survives.
Storage

4 + 3 split

Portion Meal A into 8 containers (4 days × 2 people) and Meal B into 6 (3 days × 2 people). Meal A's Thursday portions and all of Meal B go in the freezer Sunday, into the fridge Wednesday night. Anything with fresh herbs, yogurt sauce, or cucumber gets packed in a separate small container and added the day of.

Two tiers, one batch

Scaling

Cook the full batch to your ratio, then portion by weight rather than by eye — a kitchen scale is the difference between this plan working and not. Her container: ~300 g. Yours: ~450 g. Take the difference out of the rice, potatoes or lentils and leave the meat and vegetables alone; protein is the thing keeping these lunches filling at 400 kcal. Non-starchy vegetables are free volume — add more to either container without counting.

Calorie and macro figures are estimates for planning, not precise measurements — actual values shift with brands and portioning. A 1,200 kcal/day intake is at the low end of what's generally recommended for sustained use; worth running past a doctor or registered dietitian before committing to five months of it.
